1. Structure:
Start with the conclusion. Then explain the reason. Then provide the requested draft.

2. Schema:
Use these fields:
- Party:
- Court:
- Filing Type:
- Requested Relief:
- Key Facts:
- Legal Standard:
- Supporting Authority:
- Tone:

3. Syntax:
Use direct commands and complete sentences.

4. Semantics:
The request is not merely to summarize. The request is to persuade.

5. Specificity:
Use Indiana legal style. Avoid generic national legal language.

6. Scope:
Include only the issue presented. Do not add unrelated claims.

7. Source:
Use only the facts provided and verified legal authority.

8. Safeguards:
Flag any missing information. Do not fabricate case law, dates, quotes, or procedural history.

9. Style:
Use clear, confident, professional writing suitable for a trial court.

10. Sequence:
Analyze first. Draft second. Review third.

11. Stress Test:
Identify weaknesses, missing facts, and possible opposing arguments.
