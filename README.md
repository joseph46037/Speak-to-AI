# Speak to AI

## A practical prompt framework for better conversations with artificial intelligence

**Speak to AI** is a plain-language method for helping people get better answers from AI tools by giving clearer instructions, better context, and stronger quality controls.

At the center of the method is **11S**, a prompt-building framework designed to make AI requests more accurate, reusable, and easier to improve.

---

## What Is 11S?

11S is a structured way to write prompts. It helps users move from vague requests to clear, testable instructions.

The goal is simple:

> Better input creates better output.

11S gives the user a checklist for building stronger prompts before asking an AI system to draft, analyze, explain, code, summarize, or plan.

---

## The 11S Framework

| # | Element | What It Controls |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Structure | The order, layout, and organization of the request |
| 2 | Schema | The fields, variables, and reusable pattern |
| 3 | Syntax | The exact wording and command format |
| 4 | Semantics | The intended meaning behind the words |
| 5 | Specificity | The details, limits, examples, and exclusions |
| 6 | Scope | What the AI should include and avoid |
| 7 | Source | The materials, facts, law, data, or authority to rely on |
| 8 | Safeguards | Rules that prevent hallucination, overreach, or unsafe output |
| 9 | Style | Tone, audience, reading level, and rhetorical voice |
| 10 | Sequence | The order of reasoning, drafting, review, and revision |
| 11 | Stress Test | The final check for errors, gaps, contradictions, and weak assumptions |

---

## Why Speak to AI Exists

Most people do not get poor AI results because AI is useless.

They get poor results because the request is unclear.

A weak prompt often leaves the AI guessing about:

- the audience,
- the goal,
- the format,
- the source material,
- the limits,
- the tone,
- the legal, technical, or factual standard,
- and what a successful answer should look like.

Speak to AI teaches users how to reduce that guessing.

## Basic Prompt Formula

Use this simple formula when creating a prompt:

```text
Role:
You are a [specific expert role].

Task:
Create [specific deliverable].

Context:
Use the following facts, materials, and background.

Rules:
Follow these constraints and avoid these errors.

Format:
Return the answer in this structure.

Quality Check:
Before finalizing, check for missing facts, unsupported claims, and unclear wording.
